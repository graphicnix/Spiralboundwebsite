THE BAND

Weston Parker

Weston Parker started his musical career at 9 years old when he was forced into vocally battle his peers. To lose was death. After beheading his competition, he began wielding the bass guitar at 12, followed by the piano and guitar. By 14 he wrote hard rockabilly folktronica hits like "kill the kittens" and  "the cats in the bag, the bag is in in the river".

Weston was in multiple original bands writing, singing, and rocking in the following years. Weston furthered his career by winning 3 regional singing championships, having an original song featured on NBC.com, and being sent to Las Vegas to perform on ABCs' "Karaoke Battle." Upon arriving in Vegas, Weston realized this was not a battle to the death and decided the show was lame, so he insulted the host by calling him a "backstreet boy" and moved to Detroit becoming a frontman for Spiralbound.

His days consist of searching rivers for his missing cat.

Marc Lafferty

Marc Lafferty started playing bass at 13, almost on a whim to jam with his friend at the time who had just begun learning guitar.  Spent a few weeks with a lesson book in his bedroom, at which time his father decided to get him professional lessons to save his own ears from that horrendous noise By his senior year in high school, he found himself in high demand, at one point playing with 5 different bands at once…probably because he always had good snacks -

Always eager to test run new gear, and firmly believes that a musician can NEVER own too many guitars - Brews beer and mead when he’s not slapping the bass.

As a young man he dreamed of touring the world as a musician, seeing all world had to offer…well he works construction now. Does Zugg Island count as “the island life”
